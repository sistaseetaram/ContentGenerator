# Script — "The Problem Every Design Firm Has (And What It Actually Costs)"

**Duration:** ~6 min
**Format:** Talking head, no slides
**Pillar:** plain-english-ai-takes
**ICP:** Bridge 01 — design/construction firm owners
**Frame:** Problem-solving + time/money cost. Automation is the method, never the headline.

**Delivery notes:** Short sentences. Pause where marked [PAUSE]. Talk to one person — "you", not "firms." Outcome before explanation — always.

---

## HOOK (0:00–0:30)

Every design firm I've spoken to has the same problem.

A client comes in. Shows interest. You sit down, understand the brief, and start work.

First draft. Mood boards. Sometimes initial renders.

[PAUSE]

Then the client disappears.

No payment. No follow-up. Just gone.

And you're left with ten, fifteen, sometimes twenty hours of real work — done, unpaid, and unusable for anyone else.

---

## FRAME — what it actually costs (0:30–1:30)

This isn't a rare situation. It's a pattern.

And most firms I talk to have accepted it as the cost of doing business.

It isn't.

[PAUSE]

Let's put a number on it. If your time is worth ₹5,000 an hour — and for a skilled designer it should be more — a single lost pitch costs you ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh. Before a single rupee comes in.

Do that three times a year and you've worked a full month for free.

Word of mouth brings the client to the door. It doesn't protect the hours you spend after they walk in.

[PAUSE]

Here's a simple self-test. Where are your last five project reports? Not the final deliverables — the working files. The brief, the references, the key decisions you made along the way.

If that takes more than thirty seconds to answer — that's the first problem to solve.

That's the problem.

---

## THE SHIFT — what I keep seeing work (1:30–2:30)

The firms that have solved this have one thing in common.

They've changed the sequence.

Instead of doing full work to earn the client's commitment — they show enough to qualify the client first. Then the real work starts.

[PAUSE]

What does "enough" look like? A first draft. An early render. A direction — styled to how that firm specifically works, not generic, not templated.

Something that says: this is the quality you're buying. Are you in?

If yes — they proceed. If no — they've spent two hours, not twenty.

That shift is worth more than any software tool you can buy.

[PAUSE]

But most firms can't make it. Not because they don't want to — because the four things that make it possible aren't in place yet.

That's not a capability problem. It's a foundation problem. And it's exactly what I look for in every first conversation.

---

## FOUNDATION 1 — documented projects (2:30–3:15)

Past work. In one place.

Photos, drawings, key decisions from previous projects — accessible.

Because a first draft that looks like you — not like generic AI, like you — needs something to learn from.

If your past work exists only in your head, or scattered across three drives and a WhatsApp album, there is nothing to personalize from.

The firms with documented projects can generate a first draft that matches their style. The ones without are starting from zero every time.

---

## FOUNDATION 2 — organized files (3:15–3:55)

Not perfect. Just navigable.

One folder structure. A naming convention. Client, project, date.

A simple test: can someone other than you find any file in under two minutes?

If yes — ready.

If no — that's the first problem to solve. Because any system built on top of disorganized files will surface the wrong thing faster, not the right thing.

---

## FOUNDATION 3 — visible work (3:55–4:45)

This is the one that surprises people.

A real portfolio. A real LinkedIn profile. Something that shows — publicly — the quality of what you do.

Here's the mechanic: word of mouth brings the referral. But before that client calls you, they've already looked you up.

If they find nothing — or a sparse profile from five years ago — the referral loses its weight. You've already started the conversation at a discount.

And this is where pricing lives.

[PAUSE]

Every firm charging two or three times the market rate has one thing in common — when you search their name, you find something. Documented projects. Real photos. A story.

Same quality of work. Different price conversation. Because visible proof removes the doubt that justifies negotiating you down.

---

## FOUNDATION 4 — one source of project truth (4:45–5:30)

The fourth one is simple.

One agreed place where every project lives. Not WhatsApp threads and email chains and a Google Drive that three people use differently.

One place.

Because any system that helps you qualify clients faster, generate first drafts quicker, protect your time better — it needs to read your projects. Structured inputs, not scattered conversations.

This doesn't require expensive software. It requires a decision: this is where we put everything.

---

## CLOSE (5:30–6:00)

These four things — documented work, organized files, visible presence, one source of truth — this is what I check for before building anything.

Not because I'm checking boxes. Because without them, any solution I build will solve the wrong problem or solve it in the wrong place.

[PAUSE]

The goal isn't automation. The goal is getting your time back. Stopping the pattern of working for free on clients who were never serious.

That's what I build toward.

If you want to map where your firm stands on these four — the free Gem is the first conversation. Linked in bio.

*End. Clean stop. No sign-off.*
