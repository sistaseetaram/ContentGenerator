## The point

Design and construction firms keep asking about AI automation. The honest answer:
most of them aren't ready — not because AI is too complex, but because the foundation isn't there yet.

This isn't a lecture. It's the map I use in every first conversation with a new firm.
Four things that need to exist before automation makes any difference.

**Lecture guard:** Every point lands as "here's what I keep seeing work" — not "here's what you're doing wrong."
If it sounds like a school lesson, reframe until it sounds like a peer observation.

---

## What NOT to say

- ❌ "You need to fix your processes before you can use AI" — prescriptive. Sounds like a consultant.
- ❌ "Most firms get this wrong" — condescending.
- ❌ "It's actually quite simple" — dismissive.
- ❌ Any claim about ROI you can't back with a number.
- ❌ Comparisons to other tools or agencies.
- ❌ Banned words: revolutionary, game-changing, disrupt, synergy, cutting-edge, empowering.

## What to say

- ✅ "The first question I ask every firm: where are your last 5 project briefs?"
- ✅ Specific, named foundations (not vague "good processes")
- ✅ Real consequence of skipping each one — not fear, just mechanics
- ✅ "AI is a multiplier, not a fixer" — say this once, slowly
- ✅ Setu's own process: map before build. First session = just understand what exists.
- ✅ Soft close on the free Gem — one sentence, no hard sell

## Talking points

### Hook
- Don't start with "Hi I'm Sista from Setu."
- Open mid-thought: "Most design firms I talk to want AI automation. The first question I ask them is: where are your last five project briefs?"
- Let 2 seconds of silence pass after the question.
- "If that takes more than 30 seconds to answer — we start there."

### Frame — AI as multiplier
- "AI is a multiplier, not a fixer."
- "If your firm runs on organized systems, AI makes them faster. If it runs on chaos, AI makes the chaos faster."
- "The firms I've seen get the most out of automation — they had four things in place before we touched a single workflow."

### Foundation 1 — Documented projects
- "Past project documentation. Photos, specs, design decisions, client feedback — all in one place."
- "When I ask a firm for their last five projects, the answer tells me everything. If it's in one folder — ready. If it's spread across three drives, a WhatsApp album, and someone's email — not yet."
- "Automation can help you generate proposals, build a portfolio, draft case studies. But only if the raw material exists."

### Foundation 2 — Organized files
- "Not perfect. Just navigable."
- "A shared drive with a consistent naming convention. Client name. Project name. Date. That's it."
- "If a new team member can find any file in under two minutes, you're ready. If it takes 10 — the problem isn't AI."
- "An AI workflow won't find the right file faster. It'll find the wrong file faster."

### Foundation 3 — LinkedIn presence
- "This one surprises people. But clients look you up."
- "If someone refers you to a potential client, that client opens LinkedIn before they call. If your profile is empty — sparse bio, no project posts, no signal that you do what you say you do — the referral dies there."
- "AI can help you create a consistent content presence. But it needs something to work with: documented projects, real photos, real numbers."

### Foundation 4 — One source of project truth
- "Every firm I talk to has at least three systems running at once. WhatsApp for client updates. Email for invoices. A Google Drive somewhere. Some sticky notes."
- "That's not a system — that's a memory problem that gets worse as the firm grows."
- "AI agents need structured inputs. They can work with a spreadsheet, a form, a standardized folder. They can't work with 'check the thread from March, I think she said it there.'"
- "One source doesn't mean expensive software. It means one agreed place."

### Close
- "These aren't prerequisites I invented. They're what I look for in the first conversation with every firm."
- "The question isn't 'are you ready for AI?' It's 'what do you have to work with?'"
- "If you want to have that first conversation, the free Gem is the place to start. Linked in bio."
- End clean. No "hope that was helpful." No sign-off. Just stop.

## Voice reminders

- Short sentences. Under 12 words per thought on camera.
- No filler phrases: "basically", "just", "kind of", "you know what I mean"
- Outcome before explanation — lead with consequence, then mechanics
- Pause after key lines. "AI is a multiplier, not a fixer." — full stop, hold 2 seconds.
- Talk to one person. Not "design firms" — "you" and "your firm."
