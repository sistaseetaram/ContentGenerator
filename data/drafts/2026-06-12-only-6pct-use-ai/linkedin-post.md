## Pillar: Plain-English AI Takes
## Date: 2026-06-13

---

Nearly two-thirds of architects have tried AI. Roughly 1 in 5 actually run it in daily practice.

That gap is the story.

A 2026 survey of around 800 architects put the numbers on the table. 48% said poor output quality is their biggest obstacle. 70% said AI visualizations need constant professional supervision to match design intent. 33% said the tools don't fit the software they already use.

None of that is a skills problem. It's a fit problem.

The tools were built for a demo, not for a Monday morning when a client just changed the brief. Not for the real workflow — mood board to material schedule to BOQ to client-ready presentation — where every handoff has to be right.

74% expect to use AI more in the next 12 months. That intention is real.

So is the frustration.

If you run a studio — where does the gap show up most: output you can't trust, or tools that don't fit?

Firms aren't behind. The tools haven't caught up to what the work actually looks like. That's the gap I'm building Setu to close.
