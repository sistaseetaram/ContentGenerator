A client walks in, you understand the brief, you do the first draft. Mood boards, an early render, sometimes 15-20 hours of real work. Then they go quiet. No payment. No follow-up.

Most firms I talk to have accepted this as the cost of doing business. It isn't.

Put a number on it. If your time is worth ₹5,000 an hour, one lost pitch costs you ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh before a rupee comes in. Do that three times a year and you've worked a full month for free.

The firms that fixed this changed the sequence. They show enough to qualify the client first — a draft that looks like *their* work, not generic. If the client's serious, the real work starts. If not, that's 2 hours spent, not 20.

Most firms can't make that shift yet. Not a tool problem — a foundation problem. Four things have to be in place first:

— Documented past work, in one place (so a draft can look like you)
— Files anyone can navigate in under 2 minutes
— Visible public proof — this is where your pricing lives
— One source of truth, not WhatsApp + email + a shared drive

The goal isn't automation. It's getting your time back, and stopping the pattern of working free for clients who were never serious.
