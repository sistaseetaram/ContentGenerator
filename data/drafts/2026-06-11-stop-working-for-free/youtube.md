## DESCRIPTION

Most design firms lose 10-20 unpaid hours every time a client ghosts after the first draft.
Most have accepted it as the cost of doing business. It isn't — and here's the number that proves it.

If your time is worth ₹5,000 an hour, a single lost pitch costs ₹50,000 to ₹1 lakh before a rupee comes in. Do that three times a year and you've worked a full month for free.

The fix isn't software. It's changing the sequence: show enough to qualify a client first, then do the real work. If they're serious, you proceed. If not, that's 2 hours spent, not 20.

But most firms can't make that shift until four foundations are in place:

1. Documented past work, in one place — so a draft can look like you, not generic.
2. Organized, navigable files — anyone can find any file in under two minutes.
3. Visible public proof — a real portfolio and presence. This is where your pricing lives.
4. One source of truth — one place every project lives, not WhatsApp, email, and a shared drive.

This is for India's architecture, interior design, and construction firm owners who want their time back.
