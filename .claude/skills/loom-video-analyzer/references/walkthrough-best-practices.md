# Walkthrough Video Best Practices — AI Agency / Build-in-Public

Last updated: 2026-05-29. Refresh via web search at start of each analysis run.

## What's Working (as of May 2026)

These patterns consistently drive engagement on LinkedIn and YouTube for AI/automation walkthrough content:

1. **Outcome-first opening** — "$0.06 per report" in the first 5 seconds, not the last. Viewers decide in 3s whether to continue. Lead with the result.
2. **Version diffs > polished demos** — showing V1 vs. V3 beats showing only the final version. Mess is credible. Cleanup is forgettable.
3. **Specific numbers over vague claims** — "94% cost reduction" beats "significantly cheaper". Specific = credible.
4. **Short talking-head + screen combos** — face on camera for 5–10s hook, then screen share. Humanises before demonstrating.
5. **"What I got wrong" beats** — explicitly showing a failure node, a dead-end, or a three-day bug. Highest engagement moments in build-in-public content.
6. **Hard close with a question** — "What version are you on?" performs better than "Thanks for watching". Invites reply = signals LinkedIn algorithm.
7. **Native video over Loom link** — LinkedIn native video gets 3–5x more reach than embedded Loom links. Post the MP4 directly.
8. **Under 8 minutes for walkthroughs** — 5–7 min is the sweet spot. Over 10 min, drop-off is significant on LinkedIn. YouTube can sustain longer.
9. **Captions burned in** — LinkedIn's CC feature is unreliable. Burned-in captions consistently increase watch time.
10. **The "frustration is the curriculum" framing** — naming the hard parts as the real lesson resonates with builders. Avoids the "just use AI" dismissiveness.

## What's Not Working

- Long intro sequences ("Hey everyone, today I'm going to show you...") → cut to < 5s
- Apologies for early versions → own it, don't apologise
- Tool comparisons without data ("this beats Make/Zapier") → skip until you have the numbers
- Generic AI hype ("this will transform your workflow") → specifics only
- Talking about architecture without showing the canvas → always show, don't just tell
- Thumbnail-optimised video structure (over-teasing) → deliver immediately, tease the next thing

## AI Agency Walkthrough Specifics

For Setu's audience (architecture/construction firms + builders):
- Lead with the operational outcome: "your team won't touch this spreadsheet again"
- Show the before state (manual process) briefly before the automation
- Call out safety: "this runs without your team needing to touch it, and it has a cost cap"
- Price-per-run is a powerful hook for cost-sensitive clients: "$0.06 vs the human cost"
- "Weeks → minutes" is more relatable than raw cost reduction for non-technical owners

## Loom-Specific Notes

- Loom auto-captions are usable but always need manual review for: "Setu", "n8n", numbers, proper nouns
- Loom's native trim is acceptable for simple cuts; Descript for multi-cut editing
- The "Share" link in Loom goes through Loom's player — fine for DMs. For LinkedIn, export MP4.
- Loom analytics (% watched, rewatch moments) are useful post-publish feedback for next recording
